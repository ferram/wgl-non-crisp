TITLE: A Gödel Modal Logic Over Witnessed Models

----------- Overall evaluation -----------
SCORE: 1 (weak accept)
----- TEXT:
Summery:
The paper introduces a Goedel modal logic GW based on witnessed Kripke
semantics, in which the truth value of modal formulas is always
realized by an actual accessible world rather than by non-attained
infima or suprema. Building on this semantic framework, the authors
develop a constraint-based refutation calculus (CGW), prove its
soundness, completeness, and strong termination, and show that failed
proof search yields finite countermodels, establishing the finite
model property for GW. Additionally, a PSPACE decision procedure is
outlined, and an implementation is reported.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strengths:
The paper is technically solid and well-structured. The move to
witnessed semantics is well-motivated: it eliminates limit-based
phenomena in Goedel modal logics and enables a more constructive and
proof-theoretically well-behaved framework. The development of the
calculus CGW is careful and nontrivial, and the proofs of soundness,
completeness, and strong termination are convincing.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Weaknesses and Points for Improvement:
Despite its technical strength, the paper would benefit from
clarification on several conceptual aspects:

-1- The most important issue concerns the lack of a syntactic
 characterization of the logic GW. While GW is defined semantically as
 the set of formulas valid in witnessed Goedel Kripke models, the
 paper does not provide a corresponding Hilbert-style or sequent-style
 axiomatization, nor does it discuss whether such an axiomatization
 exists. In contrast, the base logic GK is explicitly introduced via
 axioms and rules. The proposed calculus CGW, although sound and
 complete, functions as a refutation and decision procedure rather
 than a standard deductive system generating theorems of the logic. As
 a result, the 'underlying logic' of GW remains only implicitly
 characterized via semantics and refutation.

-2- While the technical motivation (restoring finite model property
 and constructive semantics) is clear, the broader logical or
 practical significance could be elaborated further. In particular, it
 would be helpful to better situate GW among existing Goedel modal
 logics and explain more explicitly in which contexts this new logic
 is preferable or necessary.

-3- Although the calculus is well-designed, it is relatively complex,
with many rules and case distinctions. Some discussion on whether this
complexity is inherent or whether simplifications are possible would
be welcome.

-4- The PSPACE decision procedure is also presented at a high level; a
 slightly more detailed justification or comparison with related
 approaches could improve clarity.

SCORE: 2 (accept)
----- TEXT:
The paper studies Goedel model logic over (not necessarily crisp)
witnessed models. These are many-valued modal logics where
propositional variables and the accessibility relation take on values
in the standard Goedel algebra, i.e., the real unit
interval. Moreover, the semantics clauses for the modalities are
witnessed in the sense that the infima and suprema that need to be
determined to evaluate, respectively, a box and diamond formula at a
world are always realized among the successors of that world. The
modal logics of the classes of all Goedel models, crisp Goedel models,
witnessed and Goedel models, and crisp, witnessed Goedel modals are
shown to be distinct.

Building on their earlier work for crisp witnessed models, the authors
introduce a refutation calculus for the Goedel modal logic of (not
necessarily crisp) witnessed models. The calculus makes use of an
expanded language that can capture constraints incorporating semantic
information, rather like labelled tableaux. It is shown to be sound in
the sense that derivable sets of constraints are not satisfiable, and
complete in the sense that all non-satisfiable sets of constraints are
derivable. It is also shown to be strongly terminating. As the
countermodels extracted from derivations are finite, it follows that
the logic has the FMP.

I found the paper well-written. It should definitely be of interest to
the AiML community. The introduction contextualises the work nicely
within the broader literature. Definitions and motivations are clear
and, as far as I could see, the proofs are technically correct. I did
not find any typos or errors, except that the definition of
M-interpretation on p5 should be put in italics.

----------- Overall evaluation -----------
SCORE: 2 (accept)
----- TEXT:
Following up on authors' earlier work on Gödel modal logic, the
current paper presents a Gödel modal logic GW evaluated over
[0,1]-valued Kripke models that are witnessed, i.e. the suprema
(infima) locally evaluating diamond (box) formulas are always realized
in a successor world. The main contributions are:
- considering [0,1]-valued Kripke models with fuzzy accessibility relations (earlier work was on crisp models)
- providing constraint tableaux calculus sound, complete, and strongly terminating for GW
- providing explicit countermodel construction
- proving decidability in PSPACE, including an implementation of the algorithm.

This clearly is of interest to AiML community, the paper is well
written, and I recommend acceptance.

I have a few comments:

- one thing that the paper does not discuss further is an
  axiomatization of the logics GW^c (which is mentioned as being
  recently proposed in [10]), and GW. A comment on the difficulties in
  providing it or proving it complete would go a long way.

- Is not GW in fact PSPACE complete? Some closely related logics,
  including KG and KG^c or similar bi-Gödel modal logics are PSPACE
  complete.
